# PENCil_AAP_2024_FSP

**P**our une **É**dition **N**umérique de la **C**hronique de Baudouin d'Avesnes.

## Description

PENCil explores the modelling of textual variation in the manuscript tradition of the *Chronique de Baudouin d’Avesnes* through TEI-based scholarly encoding and editorial data structuring.

The project focuses on the representation of textual macro-variance across witnesses in order to formalise editorial knowledge about rewriting and transformation in medieval historiographical traditions. Particular attention is given to the consistency of encoding practices and to the structuring of variant phenomena as interoperable scholarly data.

By articulating editorial methodology and structured textual representation, the project contributes to ongoing reflection on the modelling of textual traditions characterised by multiple textual states.

## Research context

The *Chronique de Baudouin d’Avesnes* constitutes a relevant case study for investigating the editorial representation of macro-variation in medieval prose traditions.

The project is situated at the intersection of:

- digital scholarly editing
- philological modelling of textual variation
- TEI encoding practices
- medieval French historiographical texts
- structuring of editorial data

PENCil contributes to the development of consistent modelling frameworks for representing textual traditions characterised by high levels of variance.

## Objectives

The project aims to:

- encode textual witnesses using TEI-XML
- represent textual and narrative variation in a structured form
- formalise editorial interpretation through explicit encoding choices
- produce interoperable editorial data
- document editorial workflows for modelling textual traditions

## Repository contents

The repository gathers materials related to the editorial modelling of the *Chronique de Baudouin d’Avesnes* tradition.

### TEI encoding

TEI-XML files representing textual witnesses and editorial structures.

### editorial workflow

documentation of encoding choices and modelling strategies for representing textual variation.

### data structuring

materials illustrating how textual variance can be formalised as structured scholarly data.
